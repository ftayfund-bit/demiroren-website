Frequently Asked Questions

Velorin Fund - Moritz Tower Investment

Investment Structure

What is the total investment amount?

+

The total investment is €220,000,000 in a 100% all-equity structure. There is no debt financing, which eliminates refinancing risk and provides greater stability for investors.

What is the minimum investment amount?

+

The minimum investment is €100,000. This allows for broad investor participation while maintaining institutional-quality standards.

What is the fund structure and legal entity?

+

Velorin Fund is structured as a private real estate fund with a 10-year investment horizon plus optional 2-year extension. Detailed legal documentation including the private placement memorandum, subscription agreement, and operating agreement are provided to qualified investors.

Who can invest in the Velorin Fund?

+

The fund is open to qualified investors including:

  • Institutional investors (pension funds, insurance companies, endowments)
  • Family offices seeking core real estate exposure
  • High net worth individuals meeting minimum investment thresholds
  • Private wealth and multi-family offices

All investors must complete KYC/AML procedures and meet applicable accredited investor requirements in their jurisdiction.

Returns & Distributions

What is the annual distribution rate?

+

Investors receive a 3.75% annual cash distribution, paid quarterly. This equals €8,250,000 annually on the total €220M investment.

For a €100,000 investment, this means €3,750 per year (€937.50 per quarter).

How are distributions funded and what is the coverage?

+

Distributions are fully covered by net operating income from the property:

  • Year 1 (2025): 1.09x coverage
  • Year 2 (2026): 1.16x coverage
  • Year 5 (2029): 1.26x coverage
  • 5-Year Average: 1.19x coverage

This means the property generates 9% to 26% more income than required for distributions, providing a substantial safety cushion.

When are distributions paid?

+

Distributions are paid quarterly, within 15 business days following the end of each quarter. The first distribution is expected in Q3 2025 following fund closing and property acquisition completion.

What is the projected 10-year return?

+

Based on conservative assumptions (3% annual rent growth, exit at 3.5% cap rate):

  • 10-Year IRR: 5.2%
  • Equity Multiple: 1.66x
  • Total Return: 66.4% over 10 years

For €100,000 invested:

  • Total distributions received: €37,500
  • Share of excess cash: €8,409
  • Share of property appreciation: €20,455
  • Total return: €66,364

What happens to excess cash flow above the 3.75% distribution?

+

The property is projected to generate €7.65M in excess cash over the first 5 years beyond the required distributions. This excess can be used for:

  • Capital reserves for property improvements
  • Additional bonus distributions to investors
  • Distribution increases in later years
  • Buffer for market downturns

The specific allocation will be determined by fund management in consultation with the advisory board.

The Property

What exactly is Moritz Tower?

+

Moritz Tower is a 95-meter, 25-floor mixed-use development in Berlin's Mediaspree district. It is Berlin's tallest residential tower in Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg.

Key specifications:

  • Total floor area: 21,449 m²
  • 189 residential apartments (16,065 m²)
  • 17 office units (4,637 m²)
  • 4 retail/commercial units (748 m²)
  • 120 underground parking spaces
  • 200 storage units
  • Architect: Nöfer Architekten
  • Target: LEED Gold certification

What is the construction status?

+

The property is currently 88% complete with an expected completion date of Q2 2026. This significantly de-risks the investment compared to ground-up development, as:

  • All major structural work is complete
  • Building systems are installed
  • Only finishing work remains
  • Construction costs are largely locked in
  • Completion timeline is highly predictable

Where is the property located?

+

Address: Mühlenstraße 33, 10243 Berlin-Friedrichshain

The Mediaspree district is Berlin's premier waterfront business and lifestyle quarter, representing a €4+ billion transformation area with:

  • 40,000+ jobs created
  • 95% development complete (limited future supply)
  • Major tenants: Amazon HQ, Zalando, Mercedes-Benz
  • Mercedes-Benz Arena (17,000 capacity)
  • East Side Gallery cultural landmark

Transportation:

  • 400m walk to Ostbahnhof (100,000 daily passengers)
  • 20 minutes direct to BER Airport
  • 3 km to Alexanderplatz
  • 4.6 km to Brandenburg Gate

What is the revenue mix of the property?

+

Year 2 Stabilized Annual Revenue (€11.2M):

  • Residential: 76% (€8.5M) - 189 apartments
  • Office: 15% (€1.6M) - Grade A office space
  • Retail/Commercial: 3% (€364K) - Ground floor
  • Additional Revenues: 7% (€810K) - Parking, storage, fitness, services

This diversification across 210 total units reduces concentration risk and provides stable income streams.

What are the operating expenses?

+

Operating expenses are conservatively estimated at 15% of gross income, significantly below the typical 20-30% for mixed-use properties. This conservative estimate includes:

  • Property management (1.0%)
  • Maintenance & repairs (2.0%)
  • Utilities & energy (1.6%)
  • Insurance (0.4%)
  • Property taxes (0.6%)
  • Security/concierge (0.4%)

The new construction and energy-efficient systems (LEED Gold target) contribute to lower operating costs.

Risk & Investment Strategy

What are the main investment risks?

+

Key risks include:

  • Market Risk: Economic cycles may affect luxury residential demand
  • Occupancy Risk: Lease-up may take longer than projected
  • Rental Rate Risk: Assumed 15-20% premium may not be achieved
  • Regulatory Risk: Berlin's tenant-friendly regulations could change
  • Office Market Risk: 22% office exposure faces hybrid work trends

Risk mitigations:

  • Conservative underwriting (5% vacancy vs 2-3% market)
  • 88% construction complete reduces execution risk
  • All-equity structure eliminates financing risk
  • Pre-leasing secured with institutional tenants
  • Trophy asset status in supply-constrained market
  • Diversification across 210 units and multiple revenue streams

Why no debt financing?

+

The all-equity structure provides several strategic advantages:

  • Eliminates refinancing risk: No debt maturity concerns
  • Protects against rate volatility: No exposure to interest rate changes
  • Maximizes flexibility: No lender restrictions or covenants
  • Simplifies structure: Cleaner governance and decision-making
  • Preserves optionality: Can add leverage in future if beneficial

While leverage could amplify returns, this conservative approach prioritizes capital preservation and distribution stability for a core hold strategy.

What is the investment strategy and hold period?

+

This is a core hold investment with a 10+ year horizon. The strategy focuses on:

  • Stable quarterly cash distributions (3.75% annually)
  • Long-term capital appreciation through rent growth
  • Trophy asset in irreplaceable location
  • Value preservation over speculation

Exit strategy: Property sale anticipated after 10 years, with alternative options including refinancing for continued distributions or secondary market transfer rights (limited).

What stress testing has been done?

+

Comprehensive stress testing demonstrates distribution security:

  • 85% occupancy (vs 95% base): Coverage drops to 1.10x (still covered)
  • 20% operating costs (vs 15%): Coverage at 1.09x (still covered)
  • Zero rent growth: Coverage at 1.16x in Year 5 (still covered)
  • All three scenarios combined: Coverage at 1.03x (barely covered but sustainable)

Even under worst-case scenarios combining all negative factors, distributions remain covered.

Investment Process & Timeline

How do I invest in the Velorin Fund?

+

Investment process:

  1. Initial inquiry: Contact investor relations team
  2. Review materials: Receive and review private placement memorandum and subscription documents
  3. Due diligence: Schedule consultations, site visits, Q&A sessions
  4. Complete subscription: Submit subscription agreement, investor questionnaire, KYC/AML documents, proof of funds
  5. Capital commitment: Wire transfer to escrow (minimum €100,000)
  6. Closing: Fund reaches final close and capital deployed
  7. Quarterly reporting: Receive regular updates and distributions

Timeline: Typically 2-4 weeks from initial inquiry to completed subscription.

What is the fundraising timeline?

+
  • Q2 2025: Fund launch, investor onboarding, capital commitment period
  • Q3 2025: Final closing, property acquisition, first quarterly distribution
  • Q2 2026: Building completion, occupancy certificate, full operations
  • 2025-2029: Quarterly distributions, annual reporting
  • 2034-2035: Exit execution, final distribution, fund liquidation

Is there early liquidity or secondary market?

+

This is an illiquid, long-term investment. Limited secondary market transfer rights exist subject to:

  • Fund manager approval
  • Qualified transferee meeting investment criteria
  • Legal documentation and fees

Investors should only commit capital they can hold for the full 10+ year term. Liquidity comes primarily through quarterly distributions and final exit proceeds.

What ongoing reporting will investors receive?

+

Quarterly:

  • Financial performance reports
  • Occupancy and leasing updates
  • Distribution statements
  • Property operations summary

Annually:

  • Audited financial statements
  • Tax documentation (K-1 equivalent)
  • Investor meetings (optional attendance)
  • Market valuation updates

Tax & Legal

What are the tax implications?

+

Tax treatment varies by investor jurisdiction and status. Generally:

  • Quarterly distributions: Treated as ordinary income
  • Capital gains: Upon exit, subject to applicable capital gains rates
  • Foreign property: German withholding taxes may apply
  • Tax reporting: Annual K-1 equivalent provided

Important: Investors should consult their own tax advisors regarding specific tax treatment in their jurisdiction. Tax laws are complex and vary significantly by location and investor type.

What fees are charged?

+

Fee structure details are provided in the private placement memorandum. Typical fees for funds of this nature include:

  • Management fee: Annual fee for fund administration
  • Performance fee: Carried interest above preferred return hurdle
  • Operating expenses: Property-level costs (included in 15% opex assumption)

All fee structures are disclosed in offering documents before investment.

What governance and oversight exists?

+

The fund maintains institutional-quality governance:

  • Fund manager: Velorin Asset Management (Demirøren Nordic Capital)
  • Advisory board: Representation for investors >€5M
  • Property management: Professional third-party operators
  • Auditors: Annual financial audits by independent firm
  • Legal counsel: Ongoing legal oversight

Operating agreements detail rights, responsibilities, and decision-making processes.
